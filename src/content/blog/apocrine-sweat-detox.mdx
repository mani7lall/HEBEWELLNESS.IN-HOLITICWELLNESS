# The Biology of Intense Heat

While cold thermogenesis relies on the sympathetic spike of norepinephrine, hyperthermic conditioning (deliberate heat exposure via sauna) operates on an entirely different biophysical axis.

When the core body temperature rises by 1.5°C to 2°C, the body believes it is experiencing a life-threatening fever. This triggers an ancient, highly conserved defense mechanism: massive vasodilation and the aggressive expression of **Heat Shock Proteins (HSPs)**.

## Heat Shock Proteins: The Cellular Chaperones

Heat Shock Proteins—specifically HSP70—are molecular chaperones. As we age, proteins inside our cells become "misfolded," losing their 3D shape and turning into toxic junk that causes Alzheimer's, Parkinson's, and cardiac failure.

When you sit in a 190°F sauna for 20 minutes, you violently upregulate HSPs. These chaperones scour the cell, locate misfolded proteins, and actively physically refold them back into their correct functional shape. It is literally repairing the structural architecture of the cell in real-time.
