# The Death of a Dogma

For an entire century, neuroscience was paralyzed by a central dogma: "You are born with a fixed number of brain cells. Once they die, they are gone forever."

This has been definitively proven false. **Adult Neurogenesis**—the birth and growth of brand new neurons—occurs continuously throughout human life, primarily within the hippocampus (the brain's center for memory, learning, and emotional regulation). 

However, neurogenesis is highly fragile. It is aggressively suppressed by chronic stress (cortisol), systemic inflammation, and aging.
