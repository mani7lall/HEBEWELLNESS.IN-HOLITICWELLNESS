# The Biological Internet

For decades, biologists believed cells operated largely in isolation, communicating only via massive hormonal sweeps. We now know they are constantly whispering to each other via **Exosomes**.

Exosomes are tiny lipid vesicles, a fraction of the size of a cell, secreted into the bloodstream. They carry immense payloads of mRNA, microRNA, and precise signaling proteins. They are the internet of the human body, telling neighboring cells when to divide, when to die, and when to fight infection.

## Signal Degradation and Aging

As we age, accumulating chronic inflammation acts as "static" on this biological internet. The exosomes become corrupted. Senescent "zombie" cells release exosomes filled with toxic SASP markers, instructing younger, healthy cells to prematurely age and undergo senescence.

This corrupted intercellular signaling is the true mechanism of systemic biological decay.
