## The Target: Cytochrome C Oxidase

The primary mechanism of PBM hinges on a specific photo-acceptor enzyme within the mitochondrial membrane called **Cytochrome C Oxidase (Complex IV)**.

Under states of high stress, heavy exertion, or illness, cells produce excess Nitric Oxide (NO). This NO competitively binds to Cytochrome C Oxidase, effectively 'choking' it and preventing oxygen from binding. This halts ATP production, putting the cell into a defensive, low-energy state.

3. **Vasodilation:** The displaced Nitric Oxide is released into the surrounding blood vessels, dramatically increasing localized blood flow and carrying away inflammatory cytokines.
