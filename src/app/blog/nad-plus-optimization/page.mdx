# The Bio-Energetic Crisis

Mitochondria are the powerhouses of the cell, heavily reliant on the coenzyme Nicotinamide Adenine Dinucleotide (NAD+) to produce Adenosine Triphosphate (ATP). Without NAD+, cellular metabolism halts entirely. By age 50, humans lose roughly 50% of their NAD+ levels. 

This energy deficit is a primary driver of biological aging. When mitochondria falter, they generate excessive Reactive Oxygen Species (ROS)—free radicals that violently damage DNA and cellular structures, creating massive localized inflammation.

## The ECS Connection

By restoring mitochondrial efficiency, we relieve the ECS of this immense burden, allowing it to focus on its primary directive: systemic, multi-organ homeostasis.
